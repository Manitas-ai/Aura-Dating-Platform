User Manual

How to use Aura

Aura is built for people who are serious about finding a meaningful connection — not the fastest one. No swiping, no algorithms. Just deliberate browsing, honest profiles, and real conversations.
1

Log in

Enter your username and password on the login screen. You received these from the person who invited you. There is no email address required — just your username and a short password. Tap Sign in to enter.

Can't log in? Double-check the exact username and password. Both are case-sensitive.
2

Complete your profile

Go to Profile (bottom right). Fill in your basic info — age group, region, and a short description. Then upload up to 5 photos and set one as your primary photo.

Most importantly: fill in the Questionnaire. It has 7 chapters covering your life situation, values, lifestyle, and more. For each chapter you decide who can see it:

  • Public — anyone browsing your profile can read it
  • Flirts only — only visible after a flirt is accepted on both sides
Tip: The more honest and detailed your answers, the more meaningful your connections will be. Your questionnaire is what makes Aura different from other apps.
3

Browse on Discover

Go to Discover (bottom left). You'll see all active members. Browse at your own pace — there is no swiping or time pressure.

Use the Filter button to narrow by gender, age group, region, whether they have kids, or their desired relationship type. Tap any profile card to open the full profile, including any questionnaire chapters they've made public.

4

Add someone to your Observation List

When a profile interests you, tap the eye icon on their card, or tap Add to Observation List inside their profile. This is completely private — they won't be notified. Use it as your personal shortlist while you take your time to decide.

Find your list under Observe in the bottom navigation.

5

Propose a Flirt

When you're ready to reach out, go to your Observation List and tap Propose a flirt next to the person. Write a short personal message — up to 120 characters — about something you genuinely noticed in their profile. Then send.

  • You have a maximum of 2 attempts per person
  • If your first proposal is declined, you may try once more
  • After two declines, no further proposals to that person are possible
Tip: A specific, personal message ("I loved your reading list on Proust") works far better than a generic one. This is your first impression.
6

Handle incoming proposals

Go to Flirts (bottom navigation) and open the Pending tab. You'll see everyone who has proposed a flirt with you — their profile summary and their personal message. Take your time.

  • Tap Accept to open a private chat with them
  • Tap Decline to pass — no explanation needed
7

Start your conversation

Once both sides have accepted, a private 1-to-1 chat opens automatically in Flirts. There is no time limit, no countdown, no gamification. Write when you're ready. The conversation is yours.

Note: Once a flirt is accepted, you can also see the other person's questionnaire chapters marked as "Flirts only".
8

Leave feedback

Aura is in alpha. Your experience directly shapes what gets built next. Go to Feedback in the navigation and tell us what worked, what felt off, and what you'd like to see. Every submission is read by the team.

Design principles
Why Aura works differently
No swiping

Deliberate browsing replaces the dopamine swipe loop. You open profiles properly and read them.

No public age or city

You decide what to share and with whom. Age group and region replace exact age and location.

Personal message required

Every flirt proposal must include a note about something you noticed. No silent likes.

Private observation

You can watch a profile quietly before deciding to reach out. No pressure, no notifications.

Questionnaire depth

Your answers reveal what actually matters to you — not just what you look like.

Visibility control

You choose per chapter who can read your answers — the world, or only your accepted flirts.
