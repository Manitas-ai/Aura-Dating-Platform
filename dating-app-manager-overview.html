Strategic Context
Why this market, why now, why this segment
Market Opportunity
Online dating in Germany is growing — but the 35–50 segment is systematically underserved
The mainstream platforms — Tinder, Bumble, Hinge — are designed for users in their twenties. Their swipe-heavy, engagement-optimised models actively frustrate the 35–50 age group, who represent a disproportionately high share of disposable income, subscription willingness, and genuine relationship intent. Existing "serious" platforms such as Parship and ElitePartner suffer from dated UX, weak AI, and no mobile-native product thinking. Aura is designed to fill this gap: premium experience, modern technology, and a design philosophy built around outcomes — not engagement metrics.

  • Privacy and data discretion as a non-negotiable baseline

Weeks 1–4  ·  4 workstreams
Establishing the strategic foundation everything else is built on
Vision, market sizing, competitive landscape, and strategic positioning. A weak strategy at this stage creates compounding misalignment across all downstream phases. This phase produces the documented case for the product.
Duration3–4 weeks
Workstreams4
Deliverables8+ documents
Problem & Vision

  • Write formal vision and mission statement
  • Define success criteria and North Star metric
  • Establish guiding principles for all product decisions
Market Research

  • Demographic data: age, income, education, intent

Phase II
Target User Research
Weeks 3–7  ·  5 workstreams
Understanding the 35–50 German user before building anything
Evidence-based personas, journey maps, pain point analyses, and interview findings. This phase is what separates products users love from products that feel designed for someone else. Skipping it is the primary cause of failed product launches.
Duration4–5 weeks
Interviews15+ sessions
Deliverables12+ artefacts
User Personas (×4)
  • Anna, 42 — divorced, two children, values trust and discretion
  • Michael, 47 — senior manager, time-poor, low tolerance for poor UX
  • Julia, 38 — physician, privacy-conscious, seeks intellectual peer
  • Lukas, 36 — international career, travels regularly
  • Goals, fears, frustrations, motivations, preferred features, verbatim quote
Research Methods
  • 1:1 qualitative interviews (15 participants, 35–50 segment)
  • Online survey: 100+ responses on pain points and priorities
  • Jobs-to-be-Done framework analysis
  • Affinity mapping of recurring themes across all interviews
Journey & Pain Points
  • 9-stage user journey: from "I am ready" to "We are together"
  • Emotional curve, friction points, and drop-off moments mapped
  • Key frustrations: ghosting, fake profiles, subscription traps
  • Privacy expectations specific to 35–50 age group
Phase III
Target Market Research
Weeks 7–12  ·  4 workstreams
Deep-dive research on the 35–50 premium segment — now that we know exactly who we are building for
Phase I delivered high-level market sizing. This phase goes deeper: it analyses the competitive landscape exclusively through the lens of our defined target user. Tinder, Bumble, and Hinge are no longer relevant benchmarks here — our users do not use them. The focus shifts to Parship, ElitePartner, LoveScout24, and international premium platforms, examined in the specific context of a 35–50, educated, German-speaking audience.
Duration4–5 weeks
Workstreams4
Deliverables10+ documents
Segment-Specific Competitor Analysis
  • Parship — deep-dive: algorithm, pricing, UX, trust signals, churn, NPS
  • ElitePartner — positioning, premium tier, strengths, weaknesses
  • LoveScout24 — market share, feature gaps, user complaints
  • eDarling — psychometric matching model, pricing, conversion
  • Zweisam (50+), Gleichklang (values-based) — niche adjacencies
  • International: eharmony, Hinge (serious mode), Once — what we can learn
Market Evolution & Trends (35–50 lens)
  • How this demographic's online dating behaviour has shifted 2018–2025
  • Post-divorce and second-relationship market growth in Germany
  • AI and safety features as primary purchase drivers for 35–50 users
  • Subscription fatigue and pricing sensitivity in the premium segment
  • Trust and data privacy as conversion-critical factors (not just hygiene)
  • Growth in older-demographic mobile-first usage (iOS vs Android split)
Segment Gap Analysis & Positioning
  • Feature gap matrix: what our target users want vs what exists today
  • Price-to-value perception study across existing platforms
  • User acquisition channels that work for 35–50 (not TikTok)
  • Trust-building mechanisms absent from all current competitors
  • Emotional triggers and decision factors unique to this life stage
  • Output: refined positioning statement and differentiation manifesto
Phase IV
Product Design
Weeks 6–11  ·  6 workstreams
Defining what we build, in what order, and to what standard
Feature brainstorm, prioritisation using MoSCoW, Kano, and RICE frameworks, and a clearly defined MVP. This phase prevents the most expensive mistake in product development: building the wrong things first.
Duration4–5 weeks
Workstreams6
FrameworksMoSCoW, Kano, RICE
Feature Backlog
  • Progressive privacy reveal (unique differentiator)
  • AI-powered compatibility scoring and explanation
  • Profile depth: values, lifestyle, relationship goals
  • Video and audio introductions with AI verification
  • In-app date planning assistant
  • Anti-ghosting conversation nudges
Prioritisation
  • MoSCoW: Must Have / Should / Could / Won't
  • Kano model: identify true delighters vs baseline expectations
  • RICE scoring: Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort
  • Value vs Complexity matrix for final triage
MVP & Benchmark
  • Clear definition of what ships in v1.0
  • Feature matrix vs Tinder, Bumble, Hinge, Parship
  • Core principles: privacy-first, quality over quantity, trust over engagement
Phase V
UX & UI Design
Weeks 8–15  ·  4 workstreams
Every screen designed, tested, and approved before engineering begins
Professional UX design eliminates the most costly form of rework — changing core flows mid-development. This phase delivers wireframes for 30+ screens, a complete design system, and a tested Figma prototype. The 35–50 segment is particularly sensitive to poor UX.
Duration6–8 weeks
Screens30+ designed
User Tests3 rounds
Design System
  • Colour palette, typography scale, spacing grid
  • Full component library (buttons, inputs, cards, modals, navigation)
  • Brand identity: wordmark, visual language, tone of voice
  • Dark and light mode; WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility compliance
Wireframes — All Screens
  • Login, sign-up, identity verification, onboarding quiz
  • Profile creation, editing, photo management
  • Discovery interface, like / pass, compatibility view
  • Matches, conversation, chat, date planner
  • Settings, privacy controls, subscription, notifications
Prototype & Testing
  • High-fidelity Figma prototype with all transitions and states
  • Moderated usability testing (8 participants, target segment)
  • Iteration cycle based on findings
  • Final design sign-off before engineering handoff
Phase VI
Technical Architecture
Weeks 10–15  ·  5 workstreams
Blueprint for a scalable, secure, GDPR-native platform
Architectural decisions made now determine the platform's stability for years. This phase defines the full technology stack, data model, security design, and AI architecture. Shortcuts here create vulnerabilities and technical debt that become exponentially more expensive to fix post-launch.
Duration4–5 weeks
Workstreams5
Diagrams10+ ADRs
System & Stack
  • Mobile: Flutter or React Native (iOS + Android)
  • Backend: Spring Boot (Java) or Node.js microservices
  • Database: PostgreSQL (relational) + MongoDB (media metadata)
  • Cloud: Azure or AWS — EU (Frankfurt) region for GDPR
  • Real-time messaging via WebSockets
Data Model & Security
  • 8 entities: User, Profile, Match, Chat, Media, Subscription, Report, Notification
  • End-to-end message encryption
  • Authentication: JWT with secure refresh token rotation
  • GDPR architecture: data minimisation, right to erasure, audit trails
AI Architecture
  • Recommendation engine: collaborative + content-based filtering
  • Compatibility scoring model with explainability layer
  • AI content moderation: images, text, behavioural signals
  • Image verification and deepfake detection
  • Conversation assistant (v2.0 roadmap item)
Phase VII
Development
Weeks 13–36  ·  4 workstreams
24 weeks of engineering — six structured sprints across mobile, backend, and AI
The longest and most resource-intensive phase. Six four-week sprints deliver the complete MVP: mobile apps, backend APIs, AI integration, CI/CD pipeline, and production infrastructure. This requires sustained, uninterrupted engineering capacity.
Duration22–24 weeks
Sprints6 × 4 weeks
Engineers3–5 required
Sprint Plan
  • Sprint 1: Auth, identity verification, user profile, onboarding
  • Sprint 2: Discovery engine, compatibility scoring, like / pass, matching
  • Sprint 3: Real-time chat, notifications, media upload
  • Sprint 4: AI integration, moderation pipeline, date planner
  • Sprint 5: Subscription and payment flows, premium features
  • Sprint 6: Performance, security hardening, pre-launch polish
Engineering Standards
  • Clean architecture, domain-driven design principles
  • 90%+ unit test coverage on all core business logic
  • Code review required for every pull request
  • API documentation (OpenAPI), architecture decision records
  • Feature flags enabling staged and risk-free rollout
CI/CD & Infrastructure
  • GitHub + Azure DevOps automated pipeline
  • Automated tests triggered on every commit
  • Docker containers, Kubernetes orchestration
  • Monitoring: Datadog dashboards, alerting
  • Auto-scaling configured ahead of launch traffic spikes

A dating platform stores photos, location data, relationship status, and private conversations. Security and privacy testing are mandatory. A data breach or GDPR violation post-launch carries legal, financial, and reputational consequences that cannot be undone.

Beta Users5,500 target
SecurityExternal PenTest
Functional Testing

Performance & Security

  • GDPR compliance audit with DPO formal sign-off
Beta Programme

Strategic significance

This is a full product development initiative — not an internal tool or a prototype. It spans ten professional disciplines, requires sustained engineering effort across 24 weeks, legal counsel, professional UX design, and a structured market launch. The plan outlined here is realistic if the right conditions are in place. The following summarises what those conditions look like in practice.

Dedicated Time
Calendar Duration
~44 weeks
The plan is structured so that parallel phases reduce calendar time — but the total person-hours involved are estimated at 1,200–1,800 across all disciplines. This requires protected, focused working time — not marginal hours around other commitments.
  • Dedicated blocks for deep work: architecture, design, development
  • Regular stakeholder review points at phase transitions
  • Buffer built in for iteration cycles and unexpected complexity
  • Milestone-based progress tracking throughout

Core roles can be covered through a combination of in-house capacity, contractors, and specialist freelancers. Not all roles are required simultaneously — the Gantt determines when each is needed.

  • Full-stack engineers ×2–3 — Phase VI (Weeks 13–36)
  • Legal counsel, GDPR specialist — Phase VIII
  • Marketing manager — Phase IX
  • QA and security specialist — Phase VII
